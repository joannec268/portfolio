Project Overview

DI Circle is a web platform that helps UC Davis Design Interactive (DI) members connect with industry professionals and alumni in a more approachable, community-oriented way. The platform includes onboarding, a dashboard, community forums, event registration, connections management, and messaging. The team participated in an 8-week design sprint to address the challenge: how can we reduce the intimidation of reaching out to industry professionals and alumni, so that connecting feels genuine and accessible?

My Role

As Project Manager, I guided the team through the full design sprint, ensuring research-driven, user-centered design decisions while maintaining project momentum. I aligned the team on scope, goals, and key milestones, managed timelines, delegated tasks, and kept thorough documentation of decisions and progress. I facilitated discussions to resolve design challenges, provided constructive feedback, and mentored team members through regular 1:1s. I also acted as the primary liaison between the team, industry mentors, and Cohort Program Managers, ensuring that feedback and guidance were effectively integrated into the project.

I helped maintain focus on user needs and research insights, supporting the team in prioritizing features and defining clear deliverables. Through mentorship and feedback, I fostered a collaborative and supportive team culture while guiding critical design and research decisions. My leadership ensured that the team stayed on track, coordinated efficiently with mentors, and delivered a polished, research-informed prototype by the end of the sprint.

Impact & Outcomes

Under my guidance, the team successfully created a platform that supports authentic engagement, reduces intimidation in networking, and strengthens community connections. The project was awarded with the Audience Choice Award, highlighting the effectiveness of the team’s collaborative process and design outcomes.
